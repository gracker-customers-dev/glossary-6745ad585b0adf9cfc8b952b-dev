Corporate governance frameworks

Structures and processes that guide the management and control of corporations.

Description

Corporate governance frameworks refer to the set of rules, practices, and processes by which a company is directed and controlled. They establish the relationships among the various stakeholders in the organization, including the board of directors, management, shareholders, and other stakeholders. These frameworks are essential for ensuring accountability and transparency in a corporation's operations. They provide a blueprint for decision-making, risk management, and compliance with laws and regulations. Effective corporate governance frameworks help to align the interests of stakeholders, enhance the credibility of the company, and contribute to its long-term sustainability. Common elements of corporate governance frameworks include the board structure, roles and responsibilities, ethical standards, and mechanisms for stakeholder engagement. Different countries may have specific corporate governance codes that reflect their regulatory environments and cultural expectations, influencing how corporations operate within their jurisdictions.

Examples

  • The OECD Principles of Corporate Governance
  • The Sarbanes-Oxley Act in the United States

Additional Information

  • Corporate governance frameworks can vary significantly across different jurisdictions.
  • Strong corporate governance frameworks can lead to increased investor confidence and better access to capital.
