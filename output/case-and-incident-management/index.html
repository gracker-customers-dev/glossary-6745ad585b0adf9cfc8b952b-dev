Case and Incident Management

A structured approach to managing and resolving incidents and cases within an organization.

Description

Case and Incident Management refers to a systematic process within governance frameworks that focuses on identifying, addressing, and resolving incidents and cases that arise within an organization. Incidents can range from minor disruptions to significant events that impact operations, security, or compliance. The management process typically involves logging the incident, assessing its impact, investigating the root cause, and implementing corrective actions. Effective management ensures that incidents are dealt with promptly to minimize disruption and risk. Furthermore, case management may involve handling more complex situations that require ongoing attention, such as regulatory compliance issues or customer complaints. By establishing clear protocols and roles, organizations can improve their responsiveness and accountability, ensuring that lessons learned from incidents inform future prevention strategies. This proactive approach not only enhances operational efficiency but also strengthens the overall governance framework by promoting transparency and continuous improvement.

Examples

  • Responding to a data breach by identifying affected systems and mitigating risks.
  • Handling customer complaints through a structured process to ensure resolution and follow-up.

Additional Information

  • Incorporates tools for tracking incidents and cases over time.
  • Promotes collaboration across departments to resolve complex issues.
