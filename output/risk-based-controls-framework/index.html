Risk-based controls framework

A structured approach to identifying, assessing, and managing risks through the implementation of controls tailored to mitigate those risks.

Description

A risk-based controls framework is a systematic approach to risk management that emphasizes the identification and prioritization of risks within an organization. This framework integrates risk assessment into the governance processes, allowing organizations to allocate resources effectively and implement controls that are proportionate to the level of risk. The framework typically involves several key components: risk identification, risk assessment, control design and implementation, monitoring, and review. By focusing on risks that could impact the organization's objectives, a risk-based controls framework helps ensure that the controls in place are not only effective but also efficient. This approach fosters a culture of risk awareness and accountability, enabling organizations to adapt to changing risk landscapes. Furthermore, it supports compliance with regulatory requirements and enhances decision-making processes by providing a clear understanding of risk exposure and control effectiveness.

Examples

  • Implementation of a risk-based internal audit process that focuses on high-risk areas.
  • Development of a risk management policy that prioritizes controls based on the likelihood and impact of various risks.

Additional Information

  • Often used in conjunction with frameworks such as COSO or ISO 31000.
  • Facilitates continuous improvement by regularly updating risk assessments and controls.
