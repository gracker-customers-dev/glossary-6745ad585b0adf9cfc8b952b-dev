Business Continuity Management

Business Continuity Management (BCM) is a proactive approach to ensuring that an organization can continue operations during and after a disruption.

Description

Business Continuity Management (BCM) is a structured approach that helps organizations prepare for, respond to, and recover from disruptive incidents, such as natural disasters, cyber-attacks, or system failures. In the context of Governance Frameworks, BCM ensures that an organization has the necessary policies, procedures, and resources in place to effectively manage risks and ensure the continuity of critical business functions. It involves conducting risk assessments, developing business continuity plans, and regularly testing and reviewing these plans to ensure their effectiveness. BCM is not only about recovery but also about resilience, enabling organizations to adapt to changing circumstances and maintain operational integrity. By integrating BCM into the governance framework, organizations can demonstrate due diligence, protect their stakeholders, and enhance their reputation, while also ensuring compliance with regulatory requirements related to risk management and operational resilience.

Examples

  • Implementation of a disaster recovery plan to restore IT services after a cyber-attack.
  • Conducting regular business impact analyses to identify critical functions and necessary resources.

Additional Information

  • BCM is often part of a larger risk management and corporate governance strategy.
  • Regular training and simulations are essential to ensure all staff understand their roles in the BCM plan.
